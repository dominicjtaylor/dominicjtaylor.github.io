Terzan 5

Is Terzan 5 the remnant of a building block of the Galactic bulge?

Cosmological Context

In the Lambda Cold Dark Matter scenario, galaxy assembly takes place largely through hierarchical merging. Galaxy formation theory can be constrained from observations of stellar populations in the Milky Way (MW). Evidence for the accretion of other systems in the MW has been seen in the past, such as the Sagittarius dwarf spheroidal and recently the Gaia-Sausage-Enceladus. The precise evolutionary history of the inner few kiloparsecs of the MW ("the bulge") remains elusive, since it hosts populations from different Galactic components and is difficult to observe.

Globular clusters (GCs) are the oldest stellar systems in the MW and trace its early formation history. Some GCs situated in the bulge have been found to host stellar populations with a range of ages and metallicites. One of these is Terzan 5.

Stellar Chemistry

Meaurements of the chemical abundances of stellar populations can help to identify similarities between systems, allowing us to determine whether they formed from the same interstellar medium (ISM) of molecular gas. Terzan 5 has been shown to have similar chemical characteristics to the bulge, such as the metallicity at which the alpha-element abundance declines, which has been suggested to indicate the mass of the progenitor of the system. A large inferred mass for the progenitor of Terzan 5 has been used to suggest that it was an important contributor to the stellar mass of the bulge.

Terzan 5 vs Galactic Bulge

In my work, I compared the chemical abundances of 10 elements of Terzan 5 with those of the bulge field using a random sampling technique. I showed that they differ at statistically significant levels. Abundances between the two populations differ by more than 1 sigma in Ca, Mn, C, O, and Al, and by more than 2 sigma in Si and Mg.

I concluded that Terzan 5 is not the remnant of a major building block of the bulge. Based on predictions from hydrodynamic simulations (EAGLE), I condluded that a low mass of its progenitor would have been unable to significantly influence the average chemistry of the bulge.
